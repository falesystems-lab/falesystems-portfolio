## Case Study 1: Carrd (One-Person $1M+ SaaS)

### The Product:
Simple website builder for one-page sites

### The Numbers:
- **Founder:** AJ (solo developer)
- **Launched:** 2016
- **Revenue:** $1M+/year (estimated)
- **Users:** 2M+
- **Pricing:** $9-49/year (annual only)

### What They Did Right:

**1. Extreme Simplicity**
- Only one-page sites (niche focus)
- 5-minute setup (not 5 hours)
- No bloat (no features most people don't need)

**2. Annual Pricing**
- Cash flow upfront
- Lower perceived cost ($9/year vs $5/mo)
- Reduces churn (already paid for year)

**3. Free Tier That Works**
- 3 free sites (generous enough to be useful)
- Watermark on free (branding/virality)
- Converts ~5% to paid (industry average 2-3%)

**4. Product-Led Growth**
- Every site has "Made with Carrd" → free advertising
- Beautiful templates (people want to copy)
- Word of mouth (people share their Carrd sites)

**5. No Support Burden**
- Everything is self-service
- FAQ/docs handle 95% of questions
- Solo founder can maintain

### Lessons for Us:

✅ **Do:**
- Keep it ridiculously simple
- Annual pricing (better cash flow)
- Generous free tier (viral growth)
- Self-serve onboarding (reduce support)

❌ **Don't:**
- Try to compete with Wix/Webflow (too complex)
- Monthly pricing only (leaves money on table)
- Skimp on free tier (slows growth)

## Case Study 2: Plausible Analytics (2-Person $1M+ ARR)

### The Product:
Privacy-friendly Google Analytics alternative

### The Numbers:
- **Founders:** Uku + Marko
- **Launched:** 2019
- **Revenue:** $1M+ ARR (2023)
- **Team:** 2 people (!)
- **Pricing:** $9-99+/mo (usage-based)

### What They Did Right:

**1. Found a Hated Competitor**
- Google Analytics = bloated, privacy-invasive
- Positioned as simple + ethical
- Easy to sell against GA

**2. Transparent Everything**
- Open-source code (trust)
- Public revenue dashboard
- No secrets (authenticity)

**3. Content Marketing**
- 100+ blog posts
- SEO-optimized
- "GA alternatives" → they rank #1
- Drives massive organic traffic

**4. Indie Hacker Audience**
- Built in public (shared journey)
- Community support (word of mouth)
- Aligned values (privacy, simplicity)

**5. Fair Pricing**
- Simple tiers (easy to understand)
- No hidden fees
- Generous trial (30 days)

### Lessons for Us:

✅ **Do:**
- Find competitor everyone hates (MEE6's expensive tiers?)
- Build in public (transparency = trust)
- SEO content machine (blog = traffic)
- Community first (engage, don't just sell)

❌ **Don't:**
- Compete on features (compete on values)
- Hide pricing (kills trust)
- Ignore community (they're your advocates)

## Case Study 3: Transistor.fm ($2M+ ARR, 2 Founders)

### The Product:
Podcast hosting + analytics

### The Numbers:
- **Founders:** Justin + Jon
- **Launched:** 2017
- **Revenue:** $2M+ ARR
- **Users:** 20,000+ podcasts
- **Pricing:** $19-99+/mo

### What They Did Right:

**1. Rode a Trend**
- Podcasting exploded 2017-2020
- Early positioning = market share

**2. Exceptional Support**
- Respond to every email < 1 hour
- Personal touch (founder replies)
- Customer love = word of mouth

**3. Built in Public**
- Transparent revenue (shared on Indie Hackers)
- Shared struggles + wins
- Built audience before product

**4. Niche Positioning**
- Not "audio hosting" (too broad)
- "Podcast hosting for professionals" (specific)
- Avoided competition with big players

**5. Annual Upsells**
- Offer annual discount (2 months free)
- ~40% choose annual (cash flow boost)

### Lessons for Us:

✅ **Do:**
- Ride growing markets (Discord growth = opportunity)
- Over-deliver on support (CX = moat)
- Build audience first (pre-launch hype)
- Niche down (not "Discord bot", but "Discord moderation")

❌ **Don't:**
- Launch in declining markets
- Ignore customer support (kills retention)
- Go too broad (hard to stand out)

## Case Study 4: Nomad List ($2M+, Solo Founder)

### The Product:
Database of cities for digital nomads

### The Numbers:
- **Founder:** Pieter Levels (legendary)
- **Launched:** 2014
- **Revenue:** $2M+/year
- **Pricing:** $199 lifetime (later $99/year)

### What They Did Right:

**1. Scratched Own Itch**
- Pieter was a nomad
- Built what he needed
- Authentic product-market fit

**2. Launch Fast, Iterate**
- Built v1 in 3 weeks
- Launched half-broken (but useful)
- Improved based on feedback

**3. Community First**
- Nomad List = directory + community
- Forum, chat, meetups
- Sticky (users stayed for community)

**4. Multiple Revenue Streams**
- Memberships ($99/year)
- Job board (companies pay to list)
- Affiliate (flight/hotel bookings)
- Conferences/events

**5. Extreme Scrappiness**
- No VC funding
- Server costs < $200/mo
- Solo founder (no employees)

### Lessons for Us:

✅ **Do:**
- Ship fast, perfect later (bias to action)
- Build community (not just product)
- Multiple revenue streams (diversify)
- Stay lean (high margins)

❌ **Don't:**
- Wait for perfection (never launches)
- Ignore community (they are the product)
- Raise VC unless necessary (keep ownership)

## Case Study 5: Discord Bots (MEE6, Dyno)

### MEE6:
- **Founded:** 2016
- **Revenue:** $5-10M/year (estimated)
- **Team:** ~30 people
- **Model:** Freemium (aggressive paywall)

**What They Did:**
- Early mover (2016 = early Discord)
- Marketing-heavy (SEO, partnerships)
- Dashboard (easy setup for non-technical)

**Criticism:**
- Paywalled essential features (users hate it)
- Expensive ($12/mo)
- Seen as "greedy" by community

**Lesson:** Being first > being best (but leaves opening for competitors)

---

### Dyno:
- **Founded:** 2016
- **Revenue:** $2-4M/year (estimated)
- **Team:** ~15 people
- **Model:** Freemium (fairer than MEE6)

**What They Did:**
- Better free tier (less paywall)
- Reliable (99%+ uptime)
- Cheaper premium ($5 vs $12)

**Why It Worked:**
- Positioned as "MEE6 but fair"
- Word of mouth from unhappy MEE6 users
- Consistent quality

**Lesson:** Find angry customers of competitor, solve their pain

## Case Study 6: Danny Postma (Multiple SaaS, $50k/mo)

### The Strategy:
Build many small SaaS, diversify

### The Products:
- Headliner (podcast audiograms) - $10k/mo
- Landingfolio (landing page inspiration) - $5k/mo
- Others - various revenue

### Total: $50k/mo+ combined

### What He Did Right:

**1. Portfolio Approach**
- Not all-in on one product
- If one fails, others succeed
- Diversified risk

**2. Fast Execution**
- Launches product in 1-2 weeks
- Tests market quickly
- Kills failures, doubles down on winners

**3. SEO Focus**
- Every product has blog
- 100+ posts per product
- Traffic = free customer acquisition

**4. Templates/Inspiration Sites**
- Low-effort content (curation)
- High SEO value
- Monetize via ads + premium

### Lessons for Us:

✅ **Do:**
- Build multiple products (portfolio)
- Launch fast, kill fast
- SEO as core strategy
- Curation sites (low effort, high value)

❌ **Don't:**
- All-in on one idea (risky)
- Spend months on product no one wants
- Ignore SEO (missing free traffic)

## Case Study 7: Gumroad ($23M ARR, Small Team)

### The Product:
Sell digital products (eBooks, courses, etc.)

### The Numbers:
- **Founder:** Sahil Lavingia
- **Launched:** 2011
- **Revenue:** $23M ARR (2023)
- **Take Rate:** 10% of sales
- **Team:** ~20 people

### What They Did Right:

**1. Creator Economy Bet**
- Positioned for creators (not corporations)
- Easy setup (5 minutes)
- Beautiful storefronts

**2. No Monthly Fees**
- Pay only when you sell (10% fee)
- No risk for creators
- Aligns incentives

**3. Built-in Audience**
- Gumroad Discover (marketplace)
- Email list (cross-promotion)
- Network effects

**4. Slow + Steady**
- No hyper-growth (sustainable)
- Raised funding, later bought out (independence)
- Long-term thinking

### Lessons for Us:

✅ **Do:**
- Align pricing with customer success
- Build marketplace (network effects)
- Sustainable growth > hyper-growth

❌ **Don't:**
- Charge monthly when pay-per-use makes sense
- Ignore community (they cross-promote)

## Common Patterns (What Works)

### ✅ Do This:

1. **Niche Down**
   - Not "website builder" → "one-page site builder"
   - Not "Discord bot" → "Discord moderation bot"

2. **Find Hated Competitor**
   - Google Analytics → Plausible
   - MEE6 → Our bot

3. **Content Marketing**
   - 100+ blog posts
   - SEO = free traffic
   - Compounds over time

4. **Generous Free Tier**
   - Gets users hooked
   - Viral growth (they share)
   - Converts ~5% to paid

5. **Annual Pricing**
   - Cash flow upfront
   - Lower churn
   - Perceived as cheaper

6. **Build in Public**
   - Transparency = trust
   - Community support
   - Word of mouth

7. **Ship Fast, Iterate**
   - Launch in weeks (not months)
   - Improve based on feedback
   - Kill failures quickly

8. **Exceptional Support**
   - Reply < 1 hour
   - Personal touch
   - Customer love = retention

### ❌ Don't Do This:

1. **Wait for Perfection**
   - Never launches
   - Competitors win

2. **Ignore Community**
   - They are your marketers
   - Word of mouth = best growth

3. **Compete on Features**
   - Race to bottom
   - Compete on values, simplicity, price

4. **Paywall Everything**
   - Pisses users off (see MEE6)
   - Slows growth

5. **Monthly-Only Pricing**
   - Leaves money on table
   - Miss cash flow boost

6. **Go Too Broad**
   - Hard to stand out
   - Marketing too expensive

7. **Raise VC Unless Necessary**
   - Pressure to exit
   - Lose control
