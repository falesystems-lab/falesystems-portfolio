5 Landing Page Mistakes That Are Killing Your Conversions (And How to Fix Them)

I've built over 100 landing pages for SaaS companies, agencies, and indie hackers. From simple one-pagers to complex multi-page funnels. And I keep seeing the same mistakes over and over again.

The worst part? These mistakes are completely avoidable.

1. Slow Loading Speed (The Silent Killer)

The Problem

Your landing page takes 5 seconds to load. Maybe even 3 seconds.

You just lost 40% of your visitors.

According to Google, 53% of mobile users abandon sites that take longer than 3 seconds to load. Desktop users aren't much more patient.

Every extra second of load time = 7% drop in conversions.

How to Fix It

Immediate wins:

1. Compress images

  • Use WebP format (30-50% smaller than JPEG)
  • Tools: Squoosh.app, TinyPNG
  • Never upload images larger than needed

2. Minimize JavaScript

  • Remove unused scripts
  • Lazy load non-critical JS
  • Do you really need that chat widget on first load?

3. Use a CDN

  • Cloudflare (free tier)
  • Vercel/Netlify (free for static sites)
  • Distribute assets globally = faster load times

Target: 90+ PageSpeed score, < 2 seconds load time

2. Unclear Value Proposition

The Problem

Visitor lands on your page. Reads the headline. Still has no idea what you do.

‚ùå Headlines that tell me NOTHING:

  • "Welcome to [Company Name]"
  • "Innovative Solutions for Modern Businesses"
  • "Transform Your Digital Experience"
  • "The Future of [Vague Industry]"

If I can't understand what you do in 5 seconds, I'm gone.

How to Fix It

The 5-Second Test:

Show your landing page to someone unfamiliar with your product. Give them 5 seconds. Then ask:

  • What does this company do?
  • Who is it for?
  • What problem does it solve?

If they can't answer, your value prop is broken.

Formula for killer headlines:

[Do X] without [pain point] in [time frame]

Examples:

  • "Build landing pages without code in 30 minutes"
  • "Ship faster with the best developer experience"
  • "Simple, privacy-friendly analytics"

3. No Social Proof (The Trust Problem)

You're a new brand. Nobody knows who you are. Why should they trust you with their money?

You're asking for a transaction without building trust first. That's like proposing on a first date.

Types of social proof (use 2-3):

1. Testimonials

Specific results + photo + name (stock photos = fake)

2. Metrics/Numbers

"10,000+ users" / "$2M+ saved" / Even small numbers work

3. Logos

Client logos, "As featured in...", Certifications

4. Case Studies

Before/after results with specific metrics

üí° If you're brand new (no social proof yet):

  • Offer free work to 3-5 people in exchange for testimonials
  • Show your process (transparency = trust)
  • Build in public (tweet progress, share learnings)
  • Use personal credibility ("10 years experience")
  • Money-back guarantee (removes risk)

4. Too Many CTAs (The Paradox of Choice)

Your landing page has: "Get Started", "Request Demo", "Download Free Guide", "Contact Sales", "Watch Video", "Read Blog"...

Result: Visitor doesn't click any of them.

One landing page = One goal = One primary CTA

The CTA Hierarchy:

1. Primary CTA (1 per page)

Bright color, large, repeated 2-3 times, same action everywhere

2. Secondary CTA (optional, 0-1)

Outline button, lower commitment, less prominent

3. Navigation Links (minimal)

Logo + 1-2 links max. Consider removing nav entirely.

5. Not Mobile-Optimized (The 60% Revenue Leak)

60% of web traffic is mobile.

If your site doesn't work on mobile, you're ignoring 60% of potential customers.

Mobile-specific rules:

  • Minimum font size: 16px (anything smaller = unreadable)
  • Tap targets: 44x44px minimum (finger-sized)
  • Single column layout (no complex multi-column designs)
  • Simplify forms (fewer fields, large inputs, correct input types)
  • Test on real devices (not just Chrome DevTools)

üí° Pro tip: Design mobile-first

Start with mobile (320-375px), then scale up. Forces you to prioritize what's actually important.
